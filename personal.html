A little bit about the things I love

Programming
Probably my biggest hobby. The world of computing is massive, and I enjoy
applying the skills I have been taught in my classes to create small projects
for myself and keep my skills sharp.
Music
I played classical guitar for four years in high school. Once I got serious
about college many of my other hobbies at the time fell by the wayside as I
dove into my studies. Recently, I have begun a personal journey to bring music
back into my life.
Electronics
This summer, I was privileged to join a team at JMU X-Labs preparing for the
upcoming inter-disciplinary Autonomous Vechicles course. During my time in
that position, I got the oppurtunity to learn a bit about hardware, sensors
and electronics. I'm excited to see where this new hobby will take me in the future.
Games
Movies and television can be interesting and powerful art forms, but from a
young age I have gravitated towards games of all kinds. I have many happy memories
of laughing myself sore at a table with my family, or at my computer with my
friends. I try to view games as technical achievments, and think critically
of them as an art form. Recently in college I have been introduced to my
other major hobby, tabletop roleplaying games. Cheap, exciting, and with
infinite possibilities, I have grown very fond of these games and the shared
experiences they offer with friends.
Cooking
Before joining James Madison University as a Computer Science student, my original
plan was to transfer to Johnson and Wales as a student of Culinary Arts. Cooking is
one of the few hobbies that almost everyone can enjoy. It is easy to work with someone
that doesnt share any other interests with you, good for family time, relaxing, and
at the end of it all, you get to enjoy a delicious meal or pastry for your efforts,
and share your work with others.
